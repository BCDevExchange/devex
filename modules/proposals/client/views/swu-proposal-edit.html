3. Pricing
How pricing will be evaluated

In your proposal, for each phase submit a fixed price for your team to meet the deliverables in the time period. Depending upon the capabilities and phase, some of your team members will be needed full-time.

There are incentives in the contract to deliver early for a phase.

Your pricing will be evaluated based on a formula that compares your bid in relation to the lowest and highest bids.

From the RFP:

Fourth Gate: Price

Prices quoted will be taken to mean and deemed to be:
  1. in Canadian dollars;
  2. inclusive of duty, FOB destination, all expenses and delivery charges where applicable;
  3. exclusive of any applicable taxes; and
  4. firm for the entire term of the Contract.

To score the Proponent’s prices for Resources, the Proponent’s price will be evaluated using a Fixed Price model. The Proponents price will be evaluated based on the sum of the total price proposed for all phases. The Proponent will provide, in the Pricing tab, a Total Cost that reflects the assumed overall Resource costs that would be incurred by the Proponent across the Opportunity Phases.  Finally, the Total Cost will be competed against all valid proposals to determine the Proponent’s score for pricing.

The explanation of the Total Cost calculation is below.

Step 1: The Proponent will calculate a Total Phase Cost for each Phase
Step 2: The Province will sum the Total Resource Costs to determine the Total Proponent Cost
Step 3: The Province will calculate the number of points to award based on the following formula:

Lowest Total Proponent Cost / This Proponent’s Total Proponent Cost * Maximum Number of Points allocated to this section.

6. Code Challenge & Team Scenario
About the Code Challenge & Team Scenario
Code Challenge

Depending upon your proposal score, your team may be screened in for a Code Challenge.

A maximum of 4 teams will be invited to complete the Code Challenge. Teams will be given 5 (five) business days to complete the Code Challenge in GitHub.

The challenge will be evaluated by two independent evaluators and a static code evaluator. Those who complete the Code Challenge and meet the evaluation requirements will then proceed to the Team Scenario.

Team Scenario

Teams that successfully complete the Code Challenge will be invited to participate in a 3-hour Team Scenario.

At the start of the meeting, your team will be presented with a scenario where they can demonstrate their capabilities and how they work as a team under pressure.

This will be followed by questions of your team from the evaluation panel, including follow up questions on both the Scenario and Code Challenge.

Your entire team should participate in the Team Scenario. This can be arranged either in person or by video conference.

From the RFP:

Third Gate: Code Challenge and Team Scenario
Following the initial evaluation in the First and Second Gate, the Product Manager for the Opportunity will select and notify Proponents, based on the top four scores ‌from the First and Second Gate, to move forward to the Code Challenge and Team Scenario. Proponents will be provided a Code Challenge with a deadline provided for submission of the completed work. The Code Challenge will be made available on GitHub and remain open for 5 business days. The submitted code will be reviewed by two independent evaluators and a static code evaluator, in accordance with the Contract included here.

Following the Code Challenge, an Team Scenario will be held where the Proponents Team will demonstrate their approach to a Scenario which will be provided at the start of the session.
After your submit your proposal and the closing deadline passes, your team and questionnaire responses will be evaluated. Depending upon your score, your team may be screened in to proceed to the next phases of the procurement process.
