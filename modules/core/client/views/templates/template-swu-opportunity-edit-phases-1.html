Phases

Inception

During inception, you take your business goals and Discovery research you've done with your users and explore the potential value that a new digital product can bring. Then you prioritize features you think a Minimum Viable Product should have and try to scope out what will be required to get to an Alpha release.

You're ready for Inception if you've:
  • Done thorough Discovery work with stakeholders and potential end users
  • Have clear business objectives and a solid understanding of your users' needs
  • Have strong support from your senior executive
Deliverables
At the end of Inception, you should have:
  • Happy stakeholders with a shared vision for your digital product
  • A product backlog for the Alpha release

Proof of Concept

In this phase, you make your value propositions tangible so they can be validated. Here's where you get coding the core features you scoped out in Inception, working towards that exciting Alpha release! By the end of this phase, you should be able to plan what it will take to fully implement and maintain the product long-term.

You're ready for Proof of Concept stage if you have:
  • A clear product vision
  • A three month backlog
Deliverables
At the end of Proof of Concept, you should have:
  • Alpha release of the product
  • A build/buy/licence decision
  • Product Roadmap
  • Resourcing plan for Implementation
