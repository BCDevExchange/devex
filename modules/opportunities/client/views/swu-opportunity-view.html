Proposal Evaluation Criteria

Here's how your proposal will be scored:

  Team's Capabilities & Skills 20 points

In order for your company to submit a proposal, first you must qualify by building an interdisciplinary team of 2 or more people with all of the capabilities.

When applying for this opportunity, the team members you put forward must collectively possess all the required capabilities and be available for the time periods stated for each phase.

Your proposed team will be evaluated based upon their capabilities.

You may also see a list of preferred skills. The more of these your team has, the higher they'll score.
  Questionnaire 20 points

In your proposal, you will be asked to answer 5 or more questions about your team (we suggest that each answer should be about 300 words).

The evaluation committee will rank companies' responses to the questionnaire from strongest to weakest, without being able to see the identity of the company.
  Code Challenge25 points

Depending upon your proposal score, your team may be screened in for a Code Challenge.

Either way, you'll be notified promptly whether your team is in the running.

A maximum of 4 teams will be invited to complete the Code Challenge. Teams will be given 5 (five) business days to complete the Code Challenge in GitHub.

The challenge will be evaluated by two independent evaluators and a static code evaluator. Those who complete the Code Challenge and meet the evaluation requirements will then proceed to the Team Scenario.
  Team Scenario25 points

Teams that successfully complete the Code Challenge will be invited to participate in a 3-hour Team Scenario.

At the start of the meeting, your team will be presented with a scenario where they can demonstrate their capabilities and how they work as a team under pressure.

This will be followed by questions of your team from the evaluation panel, including follow up questions on both the Scenario and Code Challenge.

Your entire team should participate in the Team Scenario. This can be arranged either in person or by video conference.
  Pricing10 points

These are not time and materials contracts.

In your proposal, for each phase submit a fixed price for your team to meet the deliverables in the time period. Depending upon the capabilities and phase, some of your team members will be needed full-time.

There are incentives in the contract to deliver early for a phase.

Your pricing will be evaluated based on a formula that compares your bid in relation to the lowest and highest bids.
