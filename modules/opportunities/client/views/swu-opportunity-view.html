Proposal Evaluation Criteria

Here's how your proposal will be scored:

  Your Team's Capabilities & Skills 10 points

In order for your company to be considered, the team members you put forward must collectively possess all the required capabilities and be available for the time periods stated.

In the proposal, you may also see a list of preferred skills. The more of these your team has, the higher they'll score.
  The Questionnaire 20 points

Your responses to the questionnaire will be anonymized and ranked against the responses from other companies who apply.

Depending on the strength of your responses and how many other companies apply, your team may be screened out from the Interview stage.

Either way, you'll be notified shortly after the closing date whether your team is in the running.
  The Interview 60 points

If your team screens through, they'll be asked to participate in a 2-hour interview. They'll present a brief 'pitch' explaining how they would approach the business problem and work with the government product manager.

(Interviews can be facilitated in person or by video conference.)
  Pricing10 points

Your score for pricing is based on a formula that compares your bid in relation to the lowest and highest bids.
